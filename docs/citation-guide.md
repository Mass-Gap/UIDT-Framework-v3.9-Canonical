## Citing Evidence Classifications

When citing specific claims, **always include the evidence category**:

### Correct Citations:

✅ **Good:**
> "The Yang-Mills mass gap Δ = 1.710 ± 0.015 GeV (Category A: Mathematically proven) was derived analytically by Rietz (2025)."

✅ **Good:**
> "UIDT predicts a Casimir force anomaly of +0.59% at 0.66 nm (Category D: Unverified prediction, awaiting experimental test) (Rietz, 2025)."

✅ **Good:**
> "The universal invariant γ = 16.339 is phenomenologically determined (Category A-), not derived from RG first principles (Rietz, 2025, Limitation L4)."

### Incorrect Citations:

❌ **Bad:**
> "Rietz (2025) **proved** the Casimir anomaly is +0.59%."
>
> *Problem:* Category D is predicted, NOT proven.

❌ **Bad:**
> "The Hubble constant is H₀ = 70.4 km/s/Mpc (Rietz, 2025)."
>
> *Problem:* Missing Category C designation (calibrated, not independent).

❌ **Bad:**
> "γ = 16.339 is **derived** from renormalization group equations (Rietz, 2025)."
>
> *Problem:* γ is Category A- (phenomenological), see Limitation L4.

## Attribution Requirements (CC BY 4.0)

Under [CC BY 4.0 License](https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by/4.0/), you **must**:

### ✅ Required:

1. **Give Credit:**
   - Author name: Philipp Rietz
   - Title: UIDT v3.7.3
   - DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.17835200

2. **Link to License:**
   - Include link: https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by/4.0/

3. **Indicate Changes:**
   - If you modified the framework, state what was changed
   - Example: "Modified UIDT verification script to include XYZ analysis"

4. **No Endorsement Implication:**
   - Do NOT suggest the author endorses your work
   - Example: ❌ "Rietz supports our extension of UIDT to..."

### ❌ NOT Required:

- Permission to use (license is open)
- Sharing your derivative work under same license (not copyleft)
- Waiting for author approval (immediate use allowed)
