> **Goal:** Outline how an N=99-type structure could, in principle, be tested

---

## 1. UIDT Definition of N

UIDT defines

    N := N_c^2 × b_0^quenched  =  N_c^2 × (11 N_c / 3)

For SU(3) pure Yang–Mills (N_c = 3, N_f = 0):

    b_0^quenched = 11,   N_c^2 = 9  ⇒  N = 9 × 11 = 99.

This N enters vacuum density expressions and suppression factors but has no
immediate analogue in standard lattice parametrizations.[D]

---

## 2. Candidate Lattice Observables (Qualitative)

Standard lattice SU(3) studies report quantities such as:[D]

- Masses of glueballs (0++ , 2++ , ...)
- Ratios of masses to the square root of the string tension σ
- Ratios of excited to ground-state glueball masses

A conceptual N-type observable would need to be:[E]

1. **Dimensionless**
2. **Purely gluonic** (quenched)
3. **Stable** across lattice spacings and actions

Possible structures (qualitative only):

- Ratios involving three independent scales, e.g.

      O_1 = (m_{2++} / m_{0++}) × (m_{0++}^2 / σ)   (schematic)

- Combinations of Casimir-scaling factors with glueball/string ratios.

No such observable is currently known to equal 99 or cluster around 99 in
lattice studies; this is an open research problem.[D]

---

## 3. Conceptual Test Program

A concrete test of N=99 would require:[E]

1. Define one or several candidate observables O_i built from lattice
   quantities (masses, σ) whose parameter dependence mirrors the UIDT role
   of N.
2. Use existing lattice datasets to evaluate O_i and check whether they
   cluster around integer values with a preference for 99.
3. If such clustering is observed, attempt to derive O_i ≈ N_c^2 b_0 from
   continuum arguments; if not, classify N as a purely model-internal
   parameter.

---

## 4. Current Status

- No published lattice observable is known that equals or approximates 99
  in a way that would naturally identify it with UIDT’s N.[D]
- L5 remains an open prediction: N=99 is structurally natural within UIDT
  but not yet connected to Stratum I lattice data.[D]
